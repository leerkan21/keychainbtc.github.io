What is the Blockchain and How Do Keys Work?

The blockchain is like a giant notebook that's shared with the world. Every time someone sends or receives Bitcoin, a new line is written into that notebook — recording what happened. But instead of a single notebook stored in one place, this version lives on thousands of computers all around the globe.

This decentralized setup makes it very hard to cheat or tamper with transactions. Everyone has a copy, so everyone keeps each other honest. That’s one of the most important parts of how Bitcoin stays secure.

Now, how do we make sure only the right people can write their lines in the notebook? That’s where keys come in.

You have a public key, which is kind of like your Bitcoin address. It's safe to share, and anyone can use it to send you Bitcoin.

But to spend or move your Bitcoin, you need your private key. This is like a special password — known only to you — that proves you’re the real owner.

Think of it like a mailbox: your public key is the address where anyone can drop letters (or coins), but only you have the key to unlock the box and take things out.

Using these digital keys, Bitcoin lets us send and receive money securely — without needing permission from a bank. And the blockchain keeps the whole system honest, permanent, and transparent.
