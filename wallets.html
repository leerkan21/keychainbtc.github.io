Bitcoin Wallets

To use Bitcoin, you need a digital wallet. A wallet allows you to send, receive, and store your Bitcoin securely. Wallets come in different forms, each with their own pros and cons.

Types of Wallets

  • Software Wallets: Apps for mobile or desktop, great for everyday use. Examples: BlueWallet, Muun, Sparrow Wallet.
  • Hardware Wallets: Physical devices for secure long-term storage. Examples: Trezor, Ledger, Coldcard.
  • Paper Wallets: Printed keys, useful for cold storage but hard to use safely.
  • Custodial Wallets: Third-party services hold your keys (like exchanges). Less secure, but easy to use.

Security Tips

  • Always back up your seed phrase.
  • Don’t share your private keys.
  • Use hardware wallets for larger amounts.
  • Double-check addresses before sending.
